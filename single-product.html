In ophthalmic surgery, precision and stability are paramount, making these forceps essential for ophthalmic professionals. Crafted from anodized titanium, they offer both durability and lightweight handling, ensuring control and accuracy during procedures. The anti-glare finish enhances clear visualization, while the open-slot handle design facilitates safe handling. Autoclavable at 134°C, these instruments are critical for maintaining sterile conditions, thus supporting functional reliability in surgical settings. Available in two variants, they cater to the diverse needs of surgeons and clinicians.

Surgical Applications of 12mm Shaft – 115mm McPherson Angled Tying Forceps

In cataract surgery, this variant is particularly advantageous for securing sutures in the anterior segment, where its 12mm angled shaft allows for precise knot tying even in challenging anatomical positions. The extended reach and angled access of this tool facilitate meticulous handling of fine sutures, enhancing surgical outcomes by minimizing tissue trauma and ensuring secure closure in deep ocular procedures.

IOL implantation is a surgical procedure performed after cataract removal or extraction of a damaged natural lens, where a transparent artificial lens is implanted to restore clear vision.

No, IOL implantation is not limited to cataract surgery. It is also used in refractive lens exchange procedures to correct vision problems such as severe myopia, hyperopia, or presbyopia.

The procedure requires a phacoemulsification system, IOL injector and cartridge, micro-incision knives, viscoelastic agents, irrigation/aspiration handpieces, and a microscope for precision surgical visualization.

The injector and cartridge system allows the surgeon to fold and insert the IOL through a very small incision, minimizing trauma to the eye, reducing recovery time, and lowering the risk of complications.
